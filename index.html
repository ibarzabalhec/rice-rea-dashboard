The Big-Picture Approach

You have ~110 prospects across five sectors. Trying to email every one of them with the same template is a guaranteed way to get zero replies. Here's the playbook to actually convert them.

Three rules to live by

  1. Lead with Rice alumni. Every email to a firm where you have a confirmed Rice alum should mention it in the first sentence. That alone bumps reply rates 3–5x. Use the "Rice Angle" column to find them.
  2. Match the ask to the firm. Don't ask Crusoe for a $5K sponsorship on first contact and don't ask Wile Interests for a 100-person speaker night. The "Best Asks" column tells you what's realistic.
  3. One ask per email. If you want a speaker and a site visit and recruiting, send three emails over three months. Multi-ask emails read as needy and get deleted.

KPIs to Track

  • Outreach volume — emails sent per week (target: 10–15 personalized, never blast)
  • Reply rate — % of emails that get any human response (good: 20%+, great: 40%+ for warm alums)
  • Conversion rate — % of replies that become a confirmed engagement (speaker, visit, sponsor)
  • Pipeline value — dollars committed in sponsorships + estimated value of speakers/treks delivered
  • Coverage — % of Tier A prospects contacted by end of semester (target: 100%)

Cadence rules

  • Initial email → wait 7 business days → polite bump (different angle, not "just following up") → wait 14 days → final touch with a specific event date
  • Never email the same person 3 times without a yes/no. After three touches, switch contacts.
  • Track every send in this dashboard so the next REA officer doesn't restart cold.

Email Templates

Four templates, one per ask. Personalize the bracketed sections. Always include a specific date or a 2-week window — open-ended asks die in inboxes.

Template 1 — Speaker (Rice Alumni Angle)

Subject: Quick ask from a fellow Owl — Rice Real Estate Association Hi [First Name], I'm Héctor Ibarzábal, Head of External Relations for the Rice Real Estate Association. I noticed [Source: LinkedIn / Rice Business directory / news] that you're a Rice alum ([Class Year / Program]) — so first, hook 'em horns, beat the Aggies, etc. Our chapter has 100+ active undergrads and MBAs interested in CRE, and we'd love to host you for a 45-minute fireside chat this [fall / spring] semester. Two questions students would learn the most from you on: • [Specific topic tied to their recent deal / project / press] • [Career-path question relevant to current students] We can do it on campus or virtual. Looking at the weeks of [X and Y] — would either work? Thanks for considering it. Héctor Rice REA · ibarzabalhec@gmail.com

Template 2 — Site Visit / Trek

Subject: Rice Real Estate Association — site visit request, [Project Name] Hi [First Name], I'm Héctor, Head of External Relations for the Rice Real Estate Association. [Project Name] is one of the most-talked-about projects on our campus — I've heard [students / faculty member X] mention it three times this month. I'm organizing a small group (15–20 students, both undergrad and MBA) for a single afternoon site walk in [Houston / Austin / Dallas] on either [Date 1] or [Date 2]. Would your team be open to hosting us for ~90 minutes? We'd want to see the project and hear from whoever led [a specific aspect: design / capital stack / construction logistics]. Happy to handle all logistics on our end. Let me know what works. Héctor Rice REA · ibarzabalhec@gmail.com
